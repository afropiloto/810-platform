# Staking

### Overview

wPAPER is the yield-bearing wrapped version of $PAPER.

It captures real-world invoice yield generated by the protocol and distributes it to long-term token holders through a sustainable buyback model.

There is **no secondary governance token**.\
$PAPER is the sole value-capture asset.

***

## Objective

* Lock circulating supply
* Capture real-world yield
* Convert stable revenue into token demand
* Create sustainable reflexivity
* Avoid emissions-driven inflation

***

## System Architecture

### Capital Source

1. Token sale proceeds converted to stables
2. Stable treasury funds invoice lending
3. Invoices repay principal + yield in stables

The lending engine runs entirely in stablecoins.

$PAPER is never directly dumped to fund loans.

***

## Yield Flow

Invoice repayment (stablecoins)\
→ Protocol treasury\
→ Allocation split:

* Reserve buffer
* Operational costs
* Growth capital
* Buyback allocation

Buyback allocation is used to purchase $PAPER from the open market.

Purchased tokens are distributed to wPAPER holders.

No token minting required.

***

## Staking Mechanics

#### Step 1: Stake

User deposits $PAPER\
Receives wPAPER 1:1

$PAPER becomes locked.

***

#### Step 2: Yield Accrual

Protocol generates stable yield from invoice lending.

A defined percentage of net yield is allocated to market buybacks.

***

#### Step 3: Distribution

Purchased $PAPER is:

* Either auto-compounded into the staking pool (increasing wPAPER redemption value)
* Or distributed proportionally to wPAPER holders

Total supply does not inflate.

***

## NAV Logic

Protocol NAV (in stables) includes:

* Treasury stable balance
* Outstanding invoice principal
* Accrued yield
* Less loss reserves

NAV per token =\
Total stable asset value ÷ circulating $PAPER supply

As:

* Treasury compounds
* Buybacks reduce liquid supply
* Lending book scales

NAV per token increases.

***

## Reward Framework

Rewards must always satisfy:

Token value distributed ≤ Net real yield generated

If invoice net yield = 18% annually\
Maximum sustainable token APR = 18%

Anything higher requires leverage or growth expansion.

There are no emissions beyond treasury-backed buybacks.

***

## Target Yield Model

Example:

Invoice book yield (gross): 30%\
Defaults + buffer: 5%\
Ops: 5%\
Protocol reserve: 5%

Net distributable yield: 15%

Buyback allocation: 10–15%

Result:

wPAPER APR dynamically tracks real performance.

***

## Why This Works

* No inflation
* No governance token dilution
* Real asset-backed cashflow
* Continuous buy pressure
* Reduced circulating supply
* Reflexive demand loop

Real-world invoices power token demand.

Not emissions.

***

## Risk Controls

* Overcollateralised invoices
* Short duration (30–90 days)
* Default reserves
* Yield distribution capped by performance
* Transparent NAV reporting

***

1. More invoices funded
2. Higher stable yield
3. Larger buybacks
4. More tokens locked
5. Reduced float
6. Increased token demand
7. Expanded lending capacity

Scale increases yield.\
